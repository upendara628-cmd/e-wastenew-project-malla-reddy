The Human Cost of E-Waste Dumping

Behind every discarded device lies a hidden story of human suffering. This page sheds light on the people most affected by the global e-waste crisis — those who live and work amidst the toxic aftermath, often invisible to the world.

Eye-Opening Facts

  • Over 40 million tonnes of e-waste are generated globally each year.
  • Only about 17.4% is formally collected and recycled.
  • Millions of informal workers, including children, handle e-waste without protection.

The Invisible Toll on Human Health

Informal e-waste processing exposes vulnerable individuals to hazardous chemicals like lead, mercury, and flame retardants. Consequences include:

  • Severe respiratory problems and lung damage.
  • Neurological effects such as memory loss and developmental delays in children.
  • Increased rates of birth defects and chronic illnesses.
“My younger brother fell ill after working at the dump site. We didn’t know how dangerous it was. We just wanted to survive.” – A 14-year-old e-waste worker

Toxic fumes from burning e-waste contaminate the air, damaging nearby communities including schools and homes. Many affected populations lack access to healthcare and protective regulations.
