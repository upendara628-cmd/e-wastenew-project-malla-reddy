Understanding E-Waste: Types and Dangers

Protect our planet by learning how e-waste affects the environment and health — and what you can do.

Common Types of E-Waste

  • Mobile Phones & Tablets: Often contain toxic metals like lead and mercury.
  • Computers & Laptops: Include hazardous batteries and circuit boards.
  • Televisions & Displays: CRT and LCD screens with harmful phosphors and heavy metals.
  • Household Appliances: Refrigerators, microwaves with refrigerants and heavy metals.
  • Batteries: Lithium, lead-acid, and other types require careful disposal.
  • Lighting Equipment: Fluorescent bulbs containing mercury vapor.
  • Networking Equipment: Includes plastics and circuit boards with hazardous substances.

Dangers of Improper E-Waste Disposal

  • Toxic Chemical Leaks: Lead, mercury, and cadmium pollute soil and water.
  • Health Risks: Respiratory problems, neurological damage, birth defects.
  • Environmental Harm: Persistent plastics and heavy metals damage ecosystems.
  • Water & Ground Contamination: Pollutes drinking water and farmland.
  • Air Pollution: Burning e-waste releases deadly dioxins and furans.
“Millions of informal workers in e-waste recycling face health risks daily. Safe and responsible e-waste management can save lives and protect our planet.” — Environmental expert

How You Can Help

Recycle your electronics at certified centers, donate reusable devices, and spread awareness in your community. Every small action helps reduce hazardous waste and conserves valuable resources.
