Types of Recycling Units

Urban Collection Centers

Convenient centers located throughout cities where residents can drop off old electronics safely. These centers offer sorting stations and secure disposal options for batteries and cables.

They streamline recycling efforts, keeping e-waste recycling accessible and effective for busy urban populations.

Mobile Recycling Vans

These flexible, traveling units visit neighborhoods, schools, and workplaces to collect e-waste directly from the community. Ideal for outreach and awareness campaigns.

They expand recycling access to areas without permanent centers, especially underserved communities.

Industrial Processing Units

Large-scale facilities equipped with advanced machinery for dismantling, sorting, and recovering valuable materials from large volumes of electronic waste.

They maximize resource recovery and minimize environmental harm through specialized processes.

Eco-Tech Hubs

Innovative centers that merge recycling efforts with education and community engagement. They offer workshops and sustainable tech showcases.

Eco-Tech Hubs inspire change by connecting knowledge with practical action.

Retail Drop-Off Points

Partnering with electronics stores, these points allow customers to trade in or recycle old devices during purchases or repairs.

This encourages responsible consumer behavior and supports circular economy goals.

Rural Micro Units

Small-scale recycling setups in remote or underserved areas, offering local jobs and training, while managing community-specific e-waste.

Empowering rural populations and reducing hazardous dumping.

Specialized Hazardous Waste Units

Facilities dedicated to safely processing hazardous e-waste components like batteries, mercury switches, and CRT monitors.

Ensure harmful substances are neutralized or recycled without contaminating the environment.

Upcycling & Repair Workshops

Community-driven spaces where electronics are repaired or creatively transformed, reducing waste and extending product life cycles.

Encourages sustainability mindset and DIY skills among participants.
